# Notability Guide for English Wikipedia Biographies

## Overview

Notability determines whether a subject deserves their own Wikipedia article.
It is the **first check** before any drafting begins.

---

## WP:GNG — General Notability Guideline

**A topic is presumed notable if it has received significant coverage in
multiple reliable sources that are independent of the subject.**

### Three Tests

| Test | Requirement | Failure Mode |
|---|---|---|
| **Significant coverage** | Sources address the subject directly and in detail | Brief mentions, directory listings, passing references |
| **Multiple sources** | At least 2-3 independent sources | Single source, same source repeated |
| **Independent** | Not the subject, their employer, publisher, or PR | Interviews, press releases, autobiographies, company websites |

---

## Subject-Specific Notability Guidelines (SNGs)

If GNG is borderline, check for an SNG. Meeting **any one** SNG establishes
presumed notability.

### WP:ANYBIO — Any Biography

The subject is likely notable if they have received a **well-known and
significant award or honor**, or have been **inducted into a notable hall
of fame**.

**Examples that count:**
- Nobel Prize, Pulitzer Prize, Oscar, Emmy, Grammy, Tony
- Booker Prize, National Book Award
- Fields Medal, Turing Award, Wolf Prize
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, National Inventors Hall of Fame
- Academy Award, BAFTA, Golden Globe

**Examples that don't:**
- Local chamber of commerce awards
- "Employee of the Month"
- Teaching awards at a single institution
- Industry-specific awards of unclear significance

### WP:NACADEMIC — Academics and Scholars

An academic is likely notable if they meet **one or more** of:

1. **Major academic award** — Fields Medal, Turing Award, Nobel, Crafoord,
   Abel Prize, etc.
2. **Highly cited** — Exceptionally high citation count or h-index for their
   field. General guideline: h-index > 40 in most sciences, but varies
   enormously by field.
3. **Named chair** — Holds a distinguished or named chair at a major university
4. **National academy** — Elected to the National Academy of Sciences, Royal
   Society, National Academy of Engineering, etc.
5. **Editor-in-chief** of a top journal in their field
6. **Major academic society president** — President of a major learned society
7. **Highly influential work** — Their research has been widely cited as a
   major breakthrough or has had a significant impact on the field

**Not sufficient alone:**
- PhD from a good university
- Tenure at a respectable university
- Dozens of publications (normal for academics)
- Grants or funding

### WP:NARTIST — Visual Artists

An artist is likely notable if their work has:

1. Been **displayed in major museums** or galleries (MOMA, Louvre, Tate, etc.)
2. Received **significant critical attention** in reputable publications
3. Been **represented by notable galleries**
4. Won a **major art award** or competition

### WP:NCREATIVE — Creative Professionals

For authors, filmmakers, musicians, journalists, architects, etc.:

1. **Multiple independent reviews** of their work in reputable publications
2. **Major award** in their field (Booker, Oscar, Grammy, etc.)
3. Work has been **subject of significant critical analysis**
4. **Significant commercial success** documented by reliable sources

### WP:NSPORT — Athletes

An athlete is likely notable if they have:

1. Competed at the **Olympic Games**
2. Played at the **highest professional level** (NFL, NBA, MLB, Premier League, etc.)
3. Won a **major championship** or title
4. Been inducted into a **sports hall of fame**

## Common Notability Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "But they're famous in their field!" | Fame ≠ notability. Need sources, not reputation. |
| "They have a Wikipedia page in another language" | Each language Wikipedia has its own notability standards. |
| "They have 10,000 followers" | Social media following doesn't count. |
| "They won a local award" | Awards must be nationally/internationally significant. |
| "They were mentioned in the news" | Brief mentions don't count as significant coverage. |
| "Their company is notable" | Company notability ≠ founder notability. |
| "They wrote a book" | Having written a book doesn't automatically make the author notable. |

## Quick Reference Card

| Criterion | Key Question |
|---|---|
| GNG | Multiple independent sources with significant coverage? |
| ANYBIO | Major award or hall of fame? |
| NACADEMIC | Named chair, national academy, major award? |
| NARTIST | Major museum, significant critical attention? |
| NCREATIVE | Independent reviews, major award? |
| NSPORT | Olympic, highest pro level, major championship? |

## When to Flag

If none of the above criteria are clearly met, flag to the user:

> "This subject may not meet Wikipedia's notability guidelines. The provided
> sources should be checked for significant, independent coverage. I recommend
> not proceeding without additional reliable sources."
