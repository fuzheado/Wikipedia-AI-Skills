# Overcategorization Reference

Condensed from **[Wikipedia:Overcategorization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Overcategorization)** (WP:OC).
This document helps evaluate whether a proposed category violates any
overcategorization rules.

---

## Quick Decision Tree

```
Is the category based on...
├─ A non-defining characteristic?            → ❌ WP:NONDEFINING
├─ Something trivial?                        → ❌ WP:TRIVIALCAT
├─ A subjective adjective?                   → ❌ WP:SUBJECTIVECAT
├─ An arbitrary numerical cutoff?            → ❌ WP:ARBITRARYCAT
├─ A person's opinion or belief?             → ❌ WP:OPINIONCAT
├─ A performer's role, venue, or production? → ❌ WP:PERFCAT
├─ A narrow intersection of two categories?  → ❌ WP:NARROWCAT
├─ Mostly overlapping with another category? → ❌ WP:OVERLAPCAT
├─ Content not yet existing (crystal ball)?  → ❌ WP:CANCAT
├─ A person's association with someone else? → ❌ WP:OCASSOC
├─ A name-based grouping (not disambig)?     → ❌ WP:SHAREDNAME
├─ An event held at a venue, or vice versa?  → ❌ WP:OCVENUE
├─ A "miscellaneous/other" leftover?         → ❌ WP:OCMISC
├─ An award that isn't defining?             → ❌ WP:OCAWARD
└─ None of the above → ✅ Probably fine
```

## Detailed Rules

### WP:NONDEFINING — Non-defining characteristics

A category should only be created for characteristics that reliable sources
commonly and consistently use to describe the topic. A characteristic that
would NOT be appropriate in the lead section is probably not defining.

**Examples of non-defining categories that were deleted:**
- "Fast-moving drones" (subjective speed threshold)
- "Medical doctors" is fine; "Medically-skilled people" is not
- "Robots in fiction" is fine; "Fiction about robots" is vague

### WP:TRIVIALCAT — Trivial characteristics

Avoid categories for wholly peripheral characteristics.

**For biographies**, avoid categorizing by:
- Food preferences, favorite holiday destinations
- Number of tattoos
- Cause of death details (age at death, place of death)
- Unreleased or unpublished work at time of death

### WP:SUBJECTIVECAT — Subjective adjectives

Do not use these in category names:
- *Famous, popular, notable, great, important* (subjective descriptions)
- *Large, small, tall, short* (relative size)
- *Near, far* (relative distance)
- *Beautiful, evil, friendly, greedy, honest, intelligent, old, ugly, young*
  (personal traits)

### WP:ARBITRARYCAT — Arbitrary cutoffs

Avoid categories based on arbitrary numerical thresholds (e.g., "7%",
"$30,000", "100th episode"). Use lists or sortable tables instead.

Exception: Year/decade/century categories are NOT considered arbitrary.

### WP:NARROWCAT — Narrow intersections

Categories that intersect two characteristics should only exist when BOTH
parent categories are large enough that diffusion is useful. Just because an
article is in Category:A and Category:B does NOT mean Category:A and B is
needed. "Triple intersections" (A, B, and C) should generally be avoided.

### WP:OVERLAPCAT — Overlapping categories

If two categories have nearly identical inclusion criteria (e.g., "crime" and
"crime history"), merge them into one.

### WP:OCMISC — Miscellaneous catch-all categories

Do not create "Other", "Miscellaneous", or "Not otherwise specified"
categories. If articles don't fit any subcategory, leave them in the parent.

### WP:OPINIONCAT — Categories by opinion

Do not categorize people by:
- Their personal opinions (even if reliably sourced)
- Being a "supporter" or "critic" of an issue
- Personal preferences
- Allegations made *about* them by others

Exception: Being an *activist* for a cause may be defining.

### WP:PERFCAT — Performers by role, venue, or production

Do not categorize performers by:
- A specific character they portrayed (e.g., "Batman actors")
- A type of character (e.g., "Actors who played gay characters")
- A venue they performed at
- A production they appeared in
- A director they worked with

### WP:OCASSOC — Categories by association

Do not categorize people by who they are associated with professionally
(e.g., "George Martin musicians"). Performers are defined by their body of
work, not by the people they worked with.

### WP:SHAREDNAME — Categories by name

Do not group unrelated people who happen to share a name
(e.g., "People named Jackson"). Use a disambiguation page instead.

### WP:CANCAT — Crystal ball categories

Do not create categories for:
- Candidates not yet nominated
- Potential CEOs
- Possible team members
- Nominees for awards (before the award is given)

### WP:OCVENUE — Venue categories

Do not categorize:
- Locations by events held there
- Events by their hosting location

Exception: Facility use that is specific, notable, and regular
(e.g., "Major League Baseball venues").

### WP:OCAWARD — Award categories

A category of award recipients should exist only if receiving the award is
a DEFINING characteristic for the large majority of its notable recipients.

### WP:OCYEAR — Year-based categories (special case)

- Year/decade/century categories are NOT arbitrary
- People are categorized by time period only if their activity in THAT period
  is defining (a writer who lived 1850–1910 but only wrote in 1908 should be
  in "20th-century writers", not "19th-century writers")
- Do NOT categorize people by day or month of birth/death

### WP:OCLOCATION — Location categories (special case)

- Location is a valid way to diffuse large categories (e.g., "American writers
  by state")
- Place of birth is rarely defining for categorization
- Place of death is NOT normally categorized
- Place of residence of parents/relatives is never defining

## Summary: 6 Quick Checks

Before creating or using a category, run these six checks:

1. **Is the characteristic verifiable from article content?** (WP:CATV)
2. **Is the category name neutral and uncontroversial?** (WP:CATPOV)
3. **Do reliable sources consistently describe the topic this way?** (WP:CATDEF)
4. **Is this a truly defining characteristic, not trivia?** (WP:TRIVIALCAT)
5. **Is the scope appropriate — not too narrow, not overlapping?** (WP:NARROWCAT, WP:OVERLAPCAT)
6. **Is this the right namespace?** (Wikipedia vs. Commons — independent systems)
