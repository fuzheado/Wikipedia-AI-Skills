# Invalid Arguments in Notability Discussions

Common bad arguments made in AfD, PROD, and notability debates — and the policy
basis for why each fails. Knowing these helps agents avoid making the same mistakes.

---

## Arguments That Never Work

| Argument | Shortcut | Why It Fails | Counter |
|----------|----------|-------------|---------|
| "Spouse of notable person" | WP:NOTINHERITED | Relationships do not confer notability. The spouse must pass GNG or an SNG on their own. | "Notability is not inherited. Joanne Smith is John Smith's spouse, but she has no independent coverage." |
| "Child of notable person" | WP:NOTINHERITED | Same as above. Even famous children need their own coverage. | "Being Elon Musk's child does not make Baby X notable without independent significant coverage." |
| "High Google/Alexa ranking" | WP:GOOGLEHITS | Search engines cannot distinguish between useful references and trivial mentions. Data is easily gamed. | "Google hits are not a reliable measure of notability (WP:GOOGLEHITS). Show us significant coverage in reliable sources." |
| "Has an IMDb page" | WP:NOTDATABASE | IMDb is a database with low inclusion standards. It documents existence, not significance. | "IMDb is an indiscriminate database. Everyone working in film has an IMDb page; that doesn't demonstrate notability." |
| "Has a LinkedIn profile" | — | Self-published, no editorial oversight. Documents employment, not significance. | "LinkedIn is a self-published professional network. It does not establish notability." |
| "Popular on YouTube/TikTok" | WP:NWEB | Subscriber counts and views are not evidence of significant coverage in independent sources. | "Popularity on social media is not a notability criterion. We need independent reliable source coverage." |
| "Won a Grammy/Oscar" | ANYBIO | Actually **does** count — this is an example of a valid argument. But the bar is "major award," not just any award. | N/A (valid) |
| "Multiple news articles" | WP:SPIP | If all coverage stems from press releases or PR campaigns, it's not independent. Churnalism doesn't count. | "These articles all appear to be reproductions of the same press release. They are not independent." |
| "It's well-written" | WP:CONTN | Content quality does not create notability. Notability is a property of the subject, not the article. | "Even a beautifully written article doesn't make the subject notable. The question is what independent sources exist." |
| "Lots of blue links in the article" | — | Having many wikilinks to other articles doesn't establish notability. Notability must come from external sources. | "Internal links don't matter for notability. Show us external reliable sources with significant coverage." |
| "Important/famous person" | — | "Important" and "famous" are subjective. Notability is determined by verifiable evidence of coverage. | "Fame is not a substitute for significant coverage in reliable independent sources." |
| "Subject is mentioned in article X" | — | A passing mention in another article is not significant coverage of the subject itself. | "A one-line mention in an article about something else does not constitute significant coverage." |
| "Subject passes WP:GNG because it exists" | — | Existence is not evidence of notability. The subject must have attracted attention from independent sources. | "Everything exists. The question is whether independent reliable sources have written about it significantly." |
| "Article X exists, so this should too" | WP:WAX | Other articles' existence is not a valid rationale. Each article must stand on its own merits. | "Whataboutism (WP:WAX). Other articles that may not meet guidelines are not a reason to keep this one." |
| "It's in a library catalog / WorldCat" | — | Library catalog entries are bibliographic records, not significant coverage. | "WorldCat simply records that the book exists. It says nothing about the book's significance." |
| "The subject was mentioned in a textbook" | — | Textbook mentions are often passing. Must be significant, focused coverage. | "A one-paragraph mention in a textbook is routine background, not significant coverage." |
| "Local news covered it" | — | Local news can be a reliable source, but for most topics (especially national/international), local coverage alone is insufficient. | "One local newspaper article is not multiple independent sources with significant coverage." |
| "I can find sources (but don't name them)" | WP:NEXIST | Unsourced assertions that sources exist are not persuasive. Name the sources. | "After [time period], no one has provided actual sources. Merely asserting they exist is insufficient." |
| "This person has a Wikipedia article in another language" | WP:NOTPAPER | Notability standards differ across language Wikipedias. Having an article on fr.wikipedia does not guarantee one on en.wikipedia. | "Each Wikipedia has its own standards. A French Wikipedia article is not evidence for English Wikipedia notability." |
| "Delete because it's poorly written" | WP:IMPERFECT | Article quality is not a deletion rationale. Articles can be improved. | "Poor writing is fixable. Delete only if the subject is not notable, not because the prose needs work." |
| "Keep because I like it" | WP:ILIKEIT | Personal preference is not a valid argument. | "I like this topic too, but personal interest doesn't establish notability per policy." |

## Common But Subtle Traps

### The Churnalism Trap
A news article appears in a dozen outlets, but all are rewrites of the same press release.
→ This counts as **one source**, not twelve. Multiple publications of the same information
from the same origin do not create notability.

### The Routine Coverage Trap
A company announces a product launch. An athlete wins a regular-season game.
A mayor holds a press conference.
→ Routine coverage of expected events does not demonstrate notability. Look for
significant, non-routine coverage that addresses the subject in depth.

### The Database Trap
IMDb, MusicBrainz, Discogs, WorldCat, LinkedIn, Crunchbase.
→ These document that something exists. They do not demonstrate that the subject
has attracted significant attention. Inclusion in these databases is routine
and indiscriminate.

### The Primary Source Trap
Annual reports, SEC filings, CVs, company websites, court documents.
→ These are primary sources, not secondary. They can be used to verify facts in
an article, but they do not contribute to establishing notability. Notability
requires independent secondary sources.

### The Counting Trap
"I found 50 articles about this person!"
→ Count of articles is meaningless. What matters is quality: Are they independent?
Are they reliable? Do they contain significant coverage? Ten substantial, independent
feature articles are worth more than a hundred press-release rewrites.

### The Google Trap
"Search Google and you'll find thousands of results!"
→ Google search results mix significant coverage with trivial mentions, social media
profiles, directory listings, and self-published content. Search volume is not
evidence of notability.

## What DOES Work (Valid Arguments)

| Argument | Policy | Example |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Multiple independent significant reviews | GNG | "Subject received feature-length profiles in The Guardian, The Atlantic, and a book-length treatment from Oxford University Press." |
| Meets specific SNG criterion | SNG | "Subject holds a named chair at MIT (NACADEMIC #1) and is an AAAS Fellow (NACADEMIC #5)." |
| Award or honor | ANYBIO | "Subject won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction." |
| Enduring historical record | ANYBIO | "Subject is listed in the Dictionary of National Biography." |
| Chart performance | NMUSIC | "Album peaked at #5 on the Billboard 200." |
| Significant impact on field | NACADEMIC | "Subject's monograph has been cited 5,000+ times and translated into 8 languages." |
| Organizational significance | NCORP | "Subject is a Fortune 500 company with extensive independent coverage in business press and academic case studies." |
| Precedent (used carefully) | — | "Consensus at previous AfDs has consistently found similar subjects notable under the same criteria." (Must be specific, not just "other articles exist.") |
