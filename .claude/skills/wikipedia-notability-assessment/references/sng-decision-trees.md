# SNG Decision Tree Reference Cards

Quick-reference decision trees for all 13 subject-specific notability guidelines.
Each card shows: shortcut, who it applies to, the criteria, and common mistakes.

---

## 1. WP:NACADEMIC — Academics

**Who:** Scientists, researchers, philosophers, scholars (tenure-track or equivalent)

**Decision tree:** Meet ANY ONE of these 8 criteria:

```
Is the person a recognized scholar?
├── Yes → Check criteria (any 1 sufficient):
│   ├── 1. Named chair / distinguished professorship at major institution
│   ├── 2. Highly cited (h-index, total citations) — field-dependent threshold
│   ├── 3. Major academic award (Fields Medal, Nobel, Turing, MacArthur, etc.)
│   ├── 4. Editor-in-chief or editorial board of top journal in field
│   ├── 5. Fellow of prestigious learned society (NAS, Royal Society, AAAS, etc.)
│   ├── 6. Author of highly influential monograph/textbook (multiple editions, wide adoption)
│   ├── 7. Research with major impact on real-world policy or practice
│   └── 8. Substantial biographical entry in major reference work (not Who's Who)
└── No → Fall back to WP:GNG

Common mistakes:
- ❌ "Professor at University X" alone is insufficient (most professors don't meet NACADEMIC)
- ❌ Counting student evaluations, course syllabi, or routine departmental profiles
- ❌ Treating PhD as a notability criterion (it's not)
```

## 2. WP:NASTRO — Astronomical Objects

**Who:** Celestial objects, exoplanets, asteroids, comets, deep-sky objects

**Decision tree:**

```
What type of object?
├── Exoplanet confirmed by multiple detection methods → Notable
├── Named asteroid (numbered by Minor Planet Center) → Notable
├── Periodic comet → Notable
├── Deep-sky object in NGC, IC, Messier, or similar catalog → Notable
├── Notable transient (supernova, GRB) with sustained coverage → Notable
├── Proposed/speculative object (e.g., Planet Nine) with significant coverage → Notable
└── Minor planet not yet numbered → Not notable (redirect to list)

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Assuming every star in a catalog is notable (most are not — only those with significant coverage)
- ❌ Creating articles for unconfirmed exoplanet candidates
```

## 3. WP:NBOOK — Books

**Who:** Books, published works (fiction, non-fiction, academic monographs)

**Decision tree:**

```
Has the book received notable attention?
├── Pathway A (Reviews & Awards):
│   ├── Multiple independent, significant reviews in reputable publications?
│   │   (newspapers, academic journals, major literary magazines)
│   ├── OR won/was shortlisted for a major literary award?
│   │   (Pulitzer, Booker, National Book Award, Hugo, Nebula, etc.)
│   └── Yes → Notable
├── Pathway B (Sales & Impact):
│   ├── Best-seller status with verifiable, independent sales figures?
│   │   (NYT best-seller list, not "Amazon #1")
│   ├── AND the book has made a significant cultural/disciplinary impact?
│   └── Yes → Notable
└── No → Not notable (consider mention in author's article)

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Counting Amazon reviews, Goodreads ratings, or blog reviews as reliable sources
- ❌ Treating "published by major publisher" as automatically notable
- ❌ ISBN/WorldCat existence is NOT evidence of notability
```

## 4. WP:NEVENT — Events

**Who:** News events, disasters, elections, sports events, historical occurrences

**Decision tree:**

```
Does the event have lasting impact?
├── Has it had significant, enduring coverage beyond the immediate news cycle?
│   (months/years later, not just days)
├── Has it been the subject of multiple independent in-depth analyses?
│   (books, academic papers, documentaries)
├── Has it had a demonstrable lasting geographic or societal impact?
└── Any yes → Likely notable
    All no → Likely not notable (consider mentioning in broader article)

Exclusion: Routine events (annual festivals, regular sports seasons, typical weather)
are generally not notable unless the specific instance was exceptional.

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Creating articles for breaking news that hasn't demonstrated lasting impact (WP:NOTNEWS)
- ❌ Routine coverage ≠ notability (most sports matches, local events)
- ❌ Single-source events (only covered by one news outlet)
```

## 5. WP:NFILM — Films

**Who:** Films (feature, documentary, short, television film)

**Decision tree:**

```
Is the film publicly released (theatrical or major festival)?
├── No (unreleased, no confirmed production start) → Not notable
└── Yes → Check:
    ├── Multiple independent, significant reviews in reputable sources?
    │   (major newspapers, film magazines, academic film journals)
    ├── OR won major film award (Oscar, Cannes, Berlin, Venice, Sundance)?
    ├── OR notable for historical/technical significance?
    └── Any yes → Notable

Special rules:
- Films still in production: Only create article if principal photography has started
  (WP:NFF)
- Short films: Higher bar — need more substantial coverage
- TV films: Same as feature films
- Student films: Generally not notable
- Pornographic films: Same criteria apply

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Creating articles for announced-but-unproduced films
- ❌ Counting IMDb, Rotten Tomatoes aggregate, or fan sites as reliable sources
- ❌ Assuming "notable actors/director" makes the film automatically notable
```

## 6. WP:NGEO — Geographic Features

**Who:** Populated places (cities, towns, villages), natural features (mountains, rivers, lakes),
protected areas, and other named geographic locations

**Decision tree:**

```
What type of feature?
├── Populated place with a name, location, and historical existence → Notable
│   (even very small villages, ghost towns with verified information)
├── Named natural feature (mountain, river, lake, island, valley, etc.) → Notable
│   (if verifiable information exists about it)
├── Man-made feature (dam, canal, highway, bridge) → Check GNG
├── Protected area (national park, nature reserve) → Notable
├── Administrative region (county, province, state, district) → Notable
├── Proposed/hypothetical development that hasn't been built → Not notable
└── Neighborhood without defined boundaries or independent coverage → Unclear

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Creating articles for proposed housing developments or subdivisions
- ❌ Treating every named intersection or building as notable
- ❌ Assuming every place on Google Maps is notable
```

## 7. WP:NMUSIC — Music

**Who:** Musicians, composers, bands, albums, songs, music performances

**Decision tree (12 criteria, ANY ONE sufficient):**

```
Has the musician/band/album:
├── 1. Charted on national or international music chart?
├── 2. Had a recording certified gold or higher?
├── 3. Released album on major label or notable independent label?
├── 4. Won or been nominated for a major music award (Grammy, MTV, etc.)?
├── 5. Been featured on multiple national/international radio programs/playlists?
├── 6. Tour or concert that was a significant event (major venue, notable tour)?
├── 7. Been the subject of multiple independent reviews in notable publications?
├── 8. Been covered by major music magazines (Rolling Stone, NME, Mojo, etc.)?
├── 9. Had significant airplay on national radio?
├── 10. Been the subject of a biographical book or documentary?
├── 11. Served as a member of multiple notable bands?
├── 12. Composed or performed music for a notable film, TV show, or game?
└── Any yes → Notable

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Counting SoundCloud/Spotify plays, YouTube views, or Bandcamp sales
- ❌ Treating "self-released" as equivalent to a notable label
- ❌ Local band with only local coverage
```

## 8. WP:NNUMBER — Numbers

**Who:** Integers, mathematical constants, and special numbers

**Decision tree:**

```
What number?
├── 1–99 → Generally notable (most have articles)
├── Mathematical constants (π, e, φ, etc.) → Notable
├── Culturally significant numbers (42, 69, 666) → Notable
│   (but may be covered in 42 (number) style)
├── Large numbers with mathematical significance → Notable
└── Other → Very high bar; most other numbers redirect to list

Note: This is one of the most restrictive SNGs. Most numbers beyond 99
do not and should not have standalone articles.
```

## 9. WP:NCORP — Organizations and Companies

**Who:** Companies, corporations, organizations, non-profits, educational institutions,
hospitals, trade unions, advocacy groups

**Decision tree (STRICT — harder than GNG):**

```
Has the organization received significant coverage?
├── "Significant" = substantial, non-trivial, and independent
├── "Coverage" = multiple in-depth articles from reliable sources
├── Press releases, routine announcements, and directory listings do NOT count
├── Primary sources (annual reports, SEC filings, PR) do NOT count
└── Self-published material (company blog, social media) does NOT count

Specific thresholds:
├── Public company → Not automatically notable (must pass NCORP or GNG)
├── Large private company → Must pass NCORP or GNG
├── University/college → Generally notable if degree-granting, accredited
├── Hospital → Notable if major medical center or teaching hospital
├── Non-profit/NGO → Must pass NCORP or GNG
├── School (K-12) → Must pass GNG (most are not notable individually)
└── Club/society → Must have independent, significant coverage

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Assuming "Fortune 500" automatically passes (it doesn't — must have coverage)
- ❌ Using press releases, product announcements, or routine business coverage
- ❌ Counting SEC filings, annual reports, or IPO prospectuses as independent sources
- ❌ Creating articles for every high school (most fail GNG)
```

## 10. WP:BIO — People (umbrella)

**Who:** All biographical subjects. See separate references for sub-criteria.

**Decision tree (overview):**

```
Is the person alive? → Apply WP:BLP more strictly
Check WP:ANYBIO first (quickest pass):
├── Major award/honor → Notable
├── Enduring historical record → Notable
├── National biographical dictionary entry → Notable
└── None → Check specific sub-criteria:
    ├── Academic → WP:NACADEMIC
    ├── Creative professional → WP:NCREATIVE
    ├── Entertainer → WP:NENTERTAINER
    ├── Politician/Judge → WP:NPOL
    ├── Sports → WP:NSPORT
    ├── Crime-related → WP:NCRIME
    └── Other → WP:GNG
```

## 11. WP:NSPECIES — Species

**Who:** Biological species, subspecies, and taxa

**Decision tree:**

```
Is it a described species/subspecies?
├── Yes → Notable (all described species are presumed notable)
│   Exception: Fictional species (unless significant real-world coverage)
│   Exception: Cryptids without scientific recognition (redirect to list or cryptid article)
└── No (undescribed/unconfirmed) → Not notable

Comparisons between species (subspecies, higher taxa):
├── Genus → Generally notable if contains notable species
├── Family → Notable
└── Higher ranks → Notable

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Creating articles for undescribed or hypothetical species
- ❌ Articles on fictional species (Pokémon, etc.) — these belong in lists
```

## 12. WP:NSPORT — Sports

**Who:** Athletes, coaches, teams, leagues, competitions

**Decision tree (varies by sport):**

```
General principle: Competed at the highest professional level or a major
international competition.

├── Professional athlete (top-tier league in major sport) → Notable
├── Olympic athlete (competed, not just qualified) → Notable
├── World championship competitor (major sport federation) → Notable
├── Collegiate athlete (US NCAA D1) → Not inherently notable (need GNG)
├── Coach/manager at top professional level → Notable
├── Sports team in top professional league → Notable
├── Sports league → Notable
├── Season/competition → See WP:NEVENT
└── Youth/semi-professional → GNG required

Sport-specific rules:
├── Association football (soccer): Played in fully professional league or senior
    national team match (WP:NFOOTY)
├── Basketball: Played in NBA, WNBA, EuroLeague, or top-tier national league
├── Baseball: Played in MLB, NPB, KBO, or top-tier league
├── Ice hockey: Played in NHL, KHL, or top-tier national league
├── Mixed martial arts: Fought in UFC, or other major promotion with significant coverage
├── Olympic sports: Competed at Olympic Games
└── Chess: Grandmaster title or top 100 ranking (WP:NCHESS)
```

## 13. WP:NWEB — Web Content

**Who:** Websites, webcomics, podcasts, YouTube channels, internet personalities, online communities

**Decision tree (STRICT):**

```
Has the web content received significant independent coverage?
├── Multiple in-depth articles in reliable, independent sources?
│   (major newspapers, magazines, books — not blogs, not "Top 10" lists)
├── Coverage must focus on the web content itself, not merely mention it
├── Alexa rank, subscriber count, page views → NOT evidence of notability
├── Won notable award (Webby, Shorty, etc.) → May be notable
└── Influential on internet culture with academic study → May be notable

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Using subscriber count, views, or downloads as notability evidence
- ❌ Citing other websites or blogs as reliable sources
- ❌ "Mentioned in a news article about internet trends" — not significant coverage
```
